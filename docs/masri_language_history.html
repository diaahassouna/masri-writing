Language in Egypt: A Full Timeline

Every era of Egyptian history has had a popular language, an official one, and a prestige one — and its own script to go with them. Here's the full sweep, from the Old Kingdom to today.

The full picture at a glance

Popular speech, official administration, literary prestige, and script — side by side, era by era.

Egyptian Arabic has never held official status at any point on this timeline — it's always been the popular register underneath whatever language ruled or carried prestige. And Egypt has changed its script for non-linguistic reasons — conquest, religion, identity — several times before. Masri isn't a break from that pattern; it's the next chapter of it.
